SOLUTION

To build an app, ParKING, that well-informating users about the realtime availability and the price of parking slots nearby, as well as acting as a payment platform, allowing users to on the app .

DISCOVER

User Interviews

I conducted an interview with 6 targeted users.

The purpose was to figure out the painpoints and concerns on finding parking slots in the city.

The Questions

How often do you drive?

- Daily? On weekends?

- At peak hours? At off-peak hours?

When do you need to find available car parking slots?

How do you find or plan to find available parking slots?

Could you please describe it?

How long do you usually take to find available parking slots (in car parks and at the meters)?

What are the frustrations when you are finding available parking slots? Could you please describe the experience?

What are your considerations to park in a parking slot even though you have to queue up?

How often do you park at a place where it may be fined?

Why?

The Findings

Prefer pay less and walk less.

- Some prefer near car parks

- Some prefer farther meters

Drivers may take risk of being fined to park illegally in order to pay less or walk less.

Drivers preference as follows:

# Meters (Busy) Car Parks (Quiet) Illegal (Risky)
Near 1 2 or 3 5
Medium 2 or 3 4 6
Far 7 8 9

ParKING could well-inform users the available parking slots (1,2,3,4) before they go for illegal parking (5).

FINAL UI

Homescreen:
Instant realtime data

The realtime data of all nearby parking slots are instantly displayed on the homescreen, right after the app is launched. No further action required.

One-click filter:
Thumb-driven design

All action buttons are placed at the bottom. The app emphasises thumb-driven design.

It is easy as well. By only one single click, the user may switch among 3 modes.

1. “Show all” mode
2. “Meters only” mode
3. “Car parks only” mode

Users will able to concentrate more on the traffic.

Parking profile:
Thumb-driven design

Here displays more detailed information of a chosen parking slot.

Users may choose “Park” or “Direction”.

Park: to payment pageHere displays more detailed information of a chosen parking slot.
Direction: to their default navigation app (which they familar with)

Payment:
Larger primary button

Actions are reduced to at most 3 clicks.

The primary button “Pay & Park” button is significantly larger and coloured in blue to make a contrast.

All details are shown on the page. No vertical scrolling is required.

MOCKUPS

Help you find parking slots and pay

Navigated with your default app

Big buttons, simple clicks

Support multiple platforms

RETROSPECT

1. The challenges and difficulties I encountered

I have just moved to a country that I am not very familiar with. I have no idea that using parking app is quite normal, which is not my case in my home country. As I did not come across similar app before, so I did not conduct direct comparisons in early stages. I did not realise the problem until I started working on the user flow testing. I therefore made the competitive analysis again and conducting interviews again. It was a bit frustrating at first, but it was the only way to achieve a better result.

2. Things that I would do differently next time

I think I am abit too eager to finish the project quick. There are still a lot I could do better: a. To conduct more user interviews and tests because I can learn and improve a lot from users’ feedback. b. To make more detailed product comparisons, so that I could include more functionalities to my app, such as a booking system.
